Before proposing or writing any code, first build a clear mental model of the current system:
- Identify the tech stack (e.g. React, Next.js, Vue, Tailwind, shadcn/ui, etc.).
- Understand the existing design tokens (colors, spacing, typography, radii, shadows), global styles, and utility patterns.
- Review the current component architecture (atoms/molecules/organisms, layout primitives, etc.) and naming conventions.
- Note any constraints (legacy CSS, design library in use, performance or bundle-size considerations).

Ask the user focused questions to understand the user's goals. Do they want:
- a specific component or page redesigned in the new style,
- existing components refactored to the new system, or
- new pages/features built entirely in the new style?

Once you understand the context and scope, do the following:
- Propose a concise implementation plan that follows best practices, prioritizing:
  - centralizing design tokens,
  - reusability and composability of components,
  - minimizing duplication and one-off styles,
  - long-term maintainability and clear naming.
- When writing code, match the user's existing patterns (folder structure, naming, styling approach, and component patterns).
- Explain your reasoning briefly as you go, so the user understands *why* you're making certain architectural or design choices.

Always aim to:
- Preserve or improve accessibility.
- Maintain visual consistency with the provided design system.
- Leave the codebase in a cleaner, more coherent state than you found it.
- Ensure layouts are responsive and usable across devices.
- Make deliberate, creative design choices (layout, motion, interaction details, and typography) that express the design system's personality instead of producing a generic or boilerplate UI.

# Design Philosophy
**Flat Design** removes all artifice. It rejects the illusion of three-dimensionality—no drop shadows, no bevels, no realistic gradients, no textures. It relies entirely on **hierarchy through size, color, and typography**. This is not minimalism for the sake of being minimal; it's **confident reduction** that creates visual interest through pure form.

The aesthetic is **digital-native but print-inspired**: crisp edges, solid blocks of color, and a strict reliance on the grid. It communicates clarity, efficiency, and modernity. It is not "boring" or "plain"; it is **boldly reductive and graphic**. Every element exists because it is necessary. Visual interest comes from the strategic interplay of solid shapes, vibrant (but controlled) color palettes, and dynamic scale.

**Core Principles:**
1. **Zero Artificial Depth**: The Z-axis does not exist. Everything is on the same plane. However, visual hierarchy is created through scale, color contrast, and strategic layering of flat shapes.
2. **Color as Structure**: Bold background colors define sections and grouping, not lines or shadows. Color transitions are sharp, never blurred or gradual.
3. **Typography as Interface**: Text size and weight bear the load of hierarchy. Typography is geometric, bold, and demands attention.
4. **Geometric Purity**: Rectangles, circles, and squares dominate. Rounded corners are consistent and moderate. No organic blobs or complex shapes.
5. **Interactive Feedback**: Hover states are pronounced through color shifts, scale transformations, and instant transitions—never through shadow depth.
6. **Strategic Decoration**: Large, subtle geometric shapes in the background create visual interest without breaking the flat aesthetic—think poster design.

# Design Token System

## Colors (Single Palette: Light Mode)
A calm, confident palette rooted in Ateneo de Zamboanga University's blue and white identity. White-forward, academic, and clean — with no gold. High contrast is essential.

- **Background**: #FFFFFF (Pure White) — The canvas.
- **Foreground**: #2C3E55 (Slate) — Deep, readable text with a blue undertone.
- **Primary**: #1B3F8B (Royal Blue) — The "Action" color. Deep, institutional blue.
- **Primary Mid**: #4A7FC1 (Sky Blue) — Supporting blue for interactive states, icons, and accents.
- **Primary Tint**: #D6E4F5 (Light Blue) — Soft blue for tinted backgrounds, badges, and highlights.
- **Surface**: #F0F4FB (Blue White) — Secondary page backgrounds and section alternates.
- **Muted**: #F0F4FB (Blue White) — Used interchangeably with Surface for muted blocks.
- **Border**: #C8D6E8 (Cool Gray Blue) — Used sparingly for inputs and structural dividers.

## Typography
**Font Family**: 'Outfit', sans-serif.
A geometric sans-serif that mirrors the shapes of the UI.
- **Headings**: Bold (700) or Extra Bold (800). Tight letter-spacing (-0.02em).
- **Body**: Regular (400). Readable, standard spacing.
- **Labels/Buttons**: Medium (500) or SemiBold (600). Uppercase often used for labels (tracking-wider).

## Radius & Shapes
- **Radius**: rounded-md (6px) or rounded-lg (8px). Consistent throughout. Not fully rounded (pill) unless it's a tag.
- **Borders**: generally 0px. We use background colors to define edges. If a border is needed (e.g., inputs), border-2 solid color.

## Shadows & Effects
- **Shadows**: shadow-none. ABSOLUTELY NO BOX SHADOWS ON ELEMENTS.
- **Gradients**: Only subtle directional gradients for background decoration (e.g., from-[#F0F4FB] to-transparent). Never on buttons or cards. Never colorful or vibrant gradients.
- **Blur**: None on elements. No backdrop-blur effects.
- **Background Decoration**: Large geometric shapes with low opacity (bg-white/5 or bg-[#1B3F8B]/5) positioned absolutely for visual interest.

# Component Stylings

## Buttons
- **Primary**: Solid Primary (#1B3F8B) background. White text. rounded-md. Height h-14 to h-16 for good touch targets. transition-all duration-200 hover:scale-105. Hover color: #163472 (darkened Primary). No shadow.
- **Secondary**: Solid Surface background (#F0F4FB). Slate text (#2C3E55). hover:bg-[#D6E4F5] with scale effect.
- **Outline**: border-4 solid Primary (#1B3F8B). Text matches border. Transparent bg. hover:bg-[#1B3F8B] hover:text-white (fill on hover).

## Cards
- **Style**: "Color Block".
- **Appearance**: Solid background (White on Surface page, or soft tints like bg-[#D6E4F5] or bg-[#F0F4FB] for features). No shadow. No border. Padding generous (p-6 or p-8). rounded-lg.
- **Interaction**: group cursor-pointer transition-all duration-200 hover:scale-[1.02]. For tinted cards, intensify on hover with hover:bg-[#C3D8F0]. Icons within cards: group-hover:scale-110.

## Inputs
- **Normal**: Surface background (#F0F4FB). No border. Slate text (#2C3E55). rounded-md.
- **Focus**: White background. border-2 solid Primary (#1B3F8B). No glow — just the hard border.

## Section Stylings
- **Alternating Backgrounds**: Use White vs. Surface (#F0F4FB) vs. Primary (#1B3F8B) for bold section color blocks. Sharp transitions between sections.
- **Dividers**: No thin line dividers. Use whitespace or color blocks. Exception: FAQ uses thick border-2 between items.
- **Background Decoration**: Absolute-positioned geometric shapes using bg-[#1B3F8B]/5 or bg-[#4A7FC1]/10 for visual layering without depth.

# Iconography
- **Library**: lucide-react.
- **Style**: Standard to bold stroke (2px to 2.5px).
- **Treatment**: Often placed inside a solid circle — white circle on blue sections (bg-white text-[#1B3F8B]), or blue tint circle on white sections (bg-[#D6E4F5] text-[#1B3F8B]). Circle size h-14 w-14 or h-16 w-16.
- **Animation**: transition-transform duration-200 group-hover:scale-110.

# Layout & Spacing
- **Container**: max-w-7xl.
- **Grid**: Rigid. 12-column base. Elements align perfectly.
- **Spacing**: Comfortable but structured. Multiples of 4 (Tailwind default).
- **Density**: Medium. Not too airy, not too dense. "Functional".

# Motion
- **Vibe**: "Academic", "Composed", "Direct".
- **Transitions**: transition-all duration-200 for most interactions. duration-300 for larger transformations.
- **Hover**: Immediate visual feedback through:
  - Scale transformations (hover:scale-105 for buttons, hover:scale-[1.02] for cards)
  - Color shifts (darkening Primary, intensifying tints)
  - Color fills (outline buttons filling with Primary blue)
  - Icon scaling within cards (group-hover:scale-110)

# Accessibility
- **Focus Rings**: ring-2 ring-offset-2 ring-[#1B3F8B] — solid, high-contrast outlines.
- **Contrast**: White text on Primary (#1B3F8B) passes WCAG AA. Slate (#2C3E55) on white and Surface backgrounds passes AA. Avoid placing Slate text on Primary Tint without verifying contrast.

# Non-Genericness / "The Bold Factor"
- **Avoid**: "Material Design" floating cards, generic Bootstrap layouts, pale pastels everywhere.
- **Emphasize**: The "Poster" look. Treat every section like a flat graphic poster with bold color blocking rooted in the Ateneo blue and white palette.
- **Bold Choices Implemented**:
  - Large decorative geometric shapes in hero background using Primary blue at low opacity
  - Vibrant full-section color blocks (Primary Blue hero, Surface alternates, dark Slate footer)
  - Dramatic scale effects on featured cards
  - Multi-tone stat numbers using Primary Mid and Primary Tint for visual rhythm
  - Abstract geometric compositions using overlapping shapes in tinted blue
  - Pronounced hover states (scale, color intensification, fills)
  - Bold typography with tight leading and strong weight contrast
  - Thick borders (border-4 on outline buttons, border-2 on FAQ items) in Primary blue
- **Visual Interest Without Depth**: Achieved through blue color contrast, geometric layering, and scale — never shadows or gradients.
