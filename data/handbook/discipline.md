# Student Discipline

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 3 (Student Discipline)

## Purpose

The proper functioning of any social system requires regulations to govern the behavior of its members. The University adheres to the **modern concept of discipline** based on a rational approach — students are made to understand why certain modes of behavior are observed, and the University recognizes the inherent dignity and rights of every individual.

The University expects all students to obey its regulations. Erring students shall be sanctioned accordingly after **due process**, to preserve peace and order on campus.

## Penalties

The University prescribes **three categories of administrative disciplinary penalties** that may be imposed on erring students. These are commensurate with the nature and gravity of the offense.

| Penalty     | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Suspension** | A student who has committed a major offense may be suspended for a period **not exceeding 20% of the prescribed number of school days** (without CHED approval), as deemed appropriate by the College Board of Discipline. |
| **Dismissal** | A student who has committed a major offense may be **dismissed or dropped from the university's rolls**. |
| **Expulsion** | An **extreme** penalty. The offender is **barred from enrolling in all public and private schools**. Requires **prior approval of CHED** to be valid and effective. |

## Classifications of Violations

Misconduct or policy violations are classified as either **minor** or **major** offenses.

## Minor Violations

Minor violations are handled by the **Office of Student Affairs** and recorded in the student's name. Infractions deemed minor are those that:

- Are uncontested by the respondent
- Do not involve physical injury or emotional harm
- Have no evidence of malice or premeditation
- Have little or no negative implications for the University or its members

**Accumulating four (4) minor offenses within the school year is considered a MAJOR offense.**

## Board of Discipline and Due Process

The **College Board of Discipline (BOD)** is convened to ensure due process for **major violations**. The **Director of Student Affairs** facilitates these proceedings, and the **VPHE** has oversight functions.

### Composition

| Role | Voting Rights |
|------|---------------|
| Five College Deans | ✅ Yes |
| Five Student Representatives (duly elected governors) | ✅ Yes |
| Director of Student Affairs | ❌ Observer |
| One Guidance Counselor | ❌ Observer |

A **presiding officer** (chosen from those with voting rights) may vote in case of a deadlock.

## Procedure for Handling a Complaint

1. The **Office of Student Affairs (OSA)** addresses complaints of violations by informing the student/respondent **in writing within five (5) days** of receiving the complaint.
2. The respondent is asked to respond **in writing within 5 days** of notification.

### Resolution / Amicable Settlement

The **DSA conducts an initial investigation** on the merits of the complaint. If an informal settlement is reached in the initial meeting and both parties accept it, the case is immediately resolved.

If no settlement is reached, or if the actions are considered a **grave offense**, the OSA forwards the case to the **Board of Discipline (BOD)** for due process.

## Procedure for Handling Major Offenses

1. **OSA investigates** the reported case and immediately summons the offending party's parents.
2. The **Disciplinary Board sets a specific day** to try the case. The concerned student(s) are notified of the hearing, verbally or in writing.
3. The **Board renders a decision**.
4. The **VPHE informs the parent** of the party concerned, in writing, of the Board's decision.
5. **Written copies** of the decision are filed with: the Office of the President, the Director of Student Affairs, the College Registrar, the Finance Office (if dismissal), the Director of Admissions, and the Guidance Office.
6. **Appeals** should be forwarded to the **VPHE** for proper action.

## Procedure for the Hearing of a Case

1. The **DSA calls the roll** by presenting the offending party, witnesses, and others to the Board. Technical rules of evidence are NOT strictly observed, but **principles of due process are mandatory**.
2. The **DSA presents the entire case** based on the investigation.
3. The **Board members begin questioning**, then the defendant has the floor for comments or counterarguments.

**Note:** Disciplinary hearings cannot be postponed except for highly justifiable reasons ruled by the Board. The Board continues the hearings even in the complainant's absence. The **presence of the offending party is indispensable** unless waived. Waiver is presumed if the respondent fails to appear despite notice served.
