# Grading System & GPA

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 2 (Academic Regulations)

## College Grading System

AdZU employs a standardized method to assess and evaluate student performance across courses. Grades reflect academic achievement, progress, and areas for improvement, based on a combination of exams, assignments, and participation.

| Grade | Descriptor      | Percent Correct |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| 4.00  | Excellent       | 95 – 100         |
| 3.50  | Superior        | 89 – 94          |
| 3.00  | Above Average   | 83 – 88          |
| 2.50  | High Average    | 77 – 82          |
| 2.00  | Average         | 71 – 76          |
| 1.50  | Low Average     | 65 – 70          |
| 1.00  | Passed          | 60 – 64          |
| F     | Failed          | Below 60         |

## Grade Reporting

1. **Midterm and final grades** are reported in the **MyAdZU Portal** as grade points. Grades become available **10 working days** after the last day of midterm and final examinations.
2. A grade of **INC (Incomplete) for the final semester grade is NOT allowed** (Manual of Regulations for Private Schools, 2008, Sec. 83.a). Failure to submit a course requirement on the date set by the teacher results in a sanction of **0.00 for that requirement**, which is incorporated into the quarter grade.

## Grade Point Average (GPA) Computation

The Grade Point Average is computed as follows:

1. **Multiply** the grade of each subject by its number of units. (Exception: Bpract (Typing), Outreach, FFP, and NSTP/ROTC, which use Passed/Failed marks with no numerical equivalents.)
2. **Find the sum** of all the products.
3. **Find the sum** of all units.
4. **Divide** the total products by the total units. The quotient is the GPA. **Rounding off is allowed up to two (2) decimal places only.**

### Rules

- The grade for **PathFit must be included** in the computation of the GPA.
- **All academic subjects** taken by the student for credit, whether required by the program or not, are included in the GPA computation.
- Subjects with **"Failed" marks must be retaken** to comply with graduation requirements.
- A subject reported as **WP (Withdrawn with Permission)** or **Passed/Failed** is **NOT included** in the computation.
- A subject reported as **WF (Withdrawn with Failure)** is assigned a grade of **0.0** in the computation.

## Appeal for Change of Grade

A grade submitted to the Registrar can be changed only at the instructor's request and only with the approval of the **Vice President for Higher Education (VPHE)**. The form may be downloaded from the **MyAdZU portal**.

Permission may be granted **only** in the following cases (and **NOT** in cases of re-evaluation of student work):

1. **Typographical Error** in submitting online
2. **Error in Computation**
3. **Late Submission of Course Requirements**

Changes in grades are allowed **until the end of the next semester**. It is the student's responsibility to determine promptly the correctness of all grades received.

## Academic Honors

### Dean's List (Semester Honors)

| Honor          | GPA Requirement | Lowest Grade |
|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| First Honors   | At least 3.60   | 2.00         |
| Second Honors  | At least 3.30   | 2.00         |

**Conditions:**

- Students must have a **minimum academic load of 18 units** or must have taken the full semester study load prescribed by the program.
- Students must **NOT have incurred administrative disciplinary sanctions** during the semester.

### Graduation Honors

Latin Honors and Honorable Mention are granted based on GPA during the student's **entire stay in college**:

| Honor              | GPA Requirement | Lowest Grade |
|--------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Summa Cum Laude    | At least 3.80    | 2.00         |
| Magna Cum Laude    | At least 3.60    | 2.00         |
| Cum Laude          | At least 3.40    | 2.00         |
| Honorable Mention  | At least 3.30    | 2.00         |

**Conditions:**

- Students must have **completed the program within 4 years** as prescribed by the curriculum.
- Students must be **enrolled in all semesters and applicable third terms**. LOA (with official permission) is not counted toward residency for a maximum of one (1) academic year.
- Students must **NOT have incurred any administrative disciplinary sanction** from the Board of Discipline (BOD) during their entire stay at AdZU.
- **Transferees who performed well** are treated as special cases and may be granted **Honorable Mention** at the discretion of the HE Council.

### Graduation Awards

#### 1. Outstanding Graduate Student
Given to the top student of each college who exemplifies academic excellence and superior leadership qualities in the Jesuit tradition. Each college forms a special committee to vet and select its Outstanding Graduate Student. **Five (5) total** Outstanding Graduate Students are chosen — one per college. These five automatically become candidates for the **Valedictorian Award**.

**Qualifications:**

- Good moral character
- No disciplinary sanctions from the Board of Discipline (BOD) during their stay at Ateneo
- Outstanding in co- and extracurricular activities
- Actively involved in community service
- Must graduate with at least **Cum Laude** academic honor
- All unit requirements fulfilled at AdZU (except where official permission was granted to take certain subjects elsewhere)
- Enrolled in all required semesters and 3rd terms (except during officially approved LOA)

**Selection Criteria:**

| Criterion                              | Weight |
|----------------------------------------|--------|
| Superior Academic Performance (GPA)    | 40%    |
| Co- and Extra-Curricular Engagements   | 30%    |
| Character and Personality              | 30%    |

#### 2. Class Valedictorian
The most distinguished award, given to the student among the five Outstanding Graduate Students who best approximates the characteristics of an ideal Ateneo graduate — excellence in academics, leadership, community service, values, and personal character.

The **VPHE convenes an Awards Committee** to conduct a panel interview. The committee includes directors of HE Unit offices, the University Chaplain (representing the Jesuit presence), and a representative from the Ateneo College Parents Council (ACPC).

**Valedictorian Interview Criteria:**

| Criterion                                            | Weight |
|------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Intelligence/Verbal Ability (from panel interviews)  | 30%    |
| Values and Attitudes (from interview & recommendations) | 70%  |

**Final tally distribution:**

| Criterion                                            | Weight |
|------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Superior Academic Performance                        | 40%    |
| Co- and Extra-Curricular Engagements                 | 30%    |
| Character and Personality (from Awards Committee)    | 30%    |

In case of a tie, the Awards Committee reconvenes to decide.
