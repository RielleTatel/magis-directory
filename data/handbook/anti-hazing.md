# Anti-Hazing Act

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Appendix F
**Law:** Republic Act No. 11053 (June 29, 2018) — *Anti-Hazing Act of 2018*

An Act prohibiting hazing and regulating other forms of initiation rites of fraternities, sororities, and other organizations, and providing penalties for violations thereof. Amends RA 8049.

---

## Section 1 — Short Title

This Act shall be known as the **"Anti-Hazing Act of 2018"**.

## Section 2 — Definition of Terms

**(a) Hazing** — Any act that results in **physical or psychological suffering, harm, or injury** inflicted on a recruit, neophyte, applicant, or member as part of an initiation rite or practice made as a prerequisite for admission or continuing membership in a fraternity, sorority, or organization. Includes (but not limited to):

- Paddling, whipping, beating, branding
- Forced calisthenics, exposure to the weather
- Forced consumption of any food, liquor, beverage, drug, or other substance
- Any brutal treatment or forced physical activity likely to adversely affect physical/psychological health

Also includes any activity (intentional or otherwise), by one person alone or with others, that tends to **humiliate, embarrass, degrade, abuse, or endanger** by requiring a recruit, neophyte, applicant, or member to do **mental, silly, or foolish tasks**.

**(b) Initiation or Initiation Rites** — Ceremonies, practices, rituals, or other acts (formal or informal) that a person must perform or take part in to be accepted as a full-fledged member. Includes ceremonies, practices, rituals, and acts in **all stages of membership**.

**(c) Organization** — An organized body of people including but not limited to any club, association, group, fraternity, and sorority. Includes the AFP, PNP, PMA, PNPA, and other uniformed service learning institutions.

**(d) Schools** — Colleges, universities, and all other educational institutions.

## Section 3 — Prohibition on Hazing

**All forms of hazing shall be prohibited** in fraternities, sororities, and organizations in schools, including citizens military training and citizens' army training. This prohibition also applies to non-school-based, community-based, and similar organizations.

**Exceptions** (not considered hazing):

- Physical, mental, and psychological fitness training for prospective regular members of the **AFP and PNP**, as approved by the Secretary of National Defense and the National Police Commission
- Similar procedures approved by the respective heads of other uniformed learning institutions
- Customary athletic events, contests, or competitions
- Any activity that furthers a legal and legitimate objective (subject to prior submission of medical clearance/certificate)

**In no case** shall hazing be made a requirement for employment in any business or corporation.

## Section 4 — Regulation of School-Based Initiation Rites

Only initiation rites or practices that **do not constitute hazing** shall be allowed, provided:

**(a)** A **written application** to conduct initiation rites shall be made to the proper school authorities **not later than seven (7) days prior** to the scheduled initiation date.

**(b)** The written application shall indicate:
- Place and date of the initiation rites
- Names of the recruits, neophytes, or applicants to be initiated
- Manner by which the initiation rites will be conducted

**(c)** Contain an **undertaking that no harm of any kind shall be committed** by anybody during the initiation rites.

**(d)** The initiation rites shall **not last more than three (3) days**.

**(e)** Contain the names of the incumbent officers of the fraternity/sorority/organization and any person(s) who will take charge in the conduct of the initiation rites.

**(f)** Be **under oath** with a declaration that it has been posted on:
- Official school bulletin board
- Bulletin board of the office of the fraternity/sorority/organization
- Two (2) other conspicuous places in the school or organization premises

**(g)** The application shall be posted from the time of submission and shall only be removed **three (3) days after** the conduct of the initiation rites.

### Approval Guidelines

School officials must promulgate guidelines for approval or denial of applications **within 60 days** of the Act's approval. Schools have the **obligation to disapprove** applications that do not conform with requirements, with reasons clearly stated.

### Disciplinary Sanctions

School officials may impose, **after due notice and summary hearing**, disciplinary sanctions including:

- Reprimand
- Suspension
- Exclusion
- Expulsion

These apply to:

- Head and all officers of a fraternity/sorority/organization conducting initiation without approval
- All members who participated in unauthorized initiation rites (even if no hazing was conducted)
- Persons who prepared applications with false/inaccurate information (and the head/officers)

## Section 5 — Monitoring of Initiation Rites

The school head or authorized representative must assign **at least two (2) school representatives** to be present during the initiation. Their duties:

- See to it that no hazing is conducted
- Document the entire proceedings
- Make a report of the initiation rites to school officials

**If hazing is committed despite their presence**, no liability attaches to them **unless proven that they failed to perform an overt act to prevent or stop the commission**.

## Section 6 — Registration of Fraternities, Sororities, and Other Organizations

**All existing fraternities/sororities/organizations** not created by the school but with student members (or plans to recruit students) must register with proper school authorities **before conducting activities** (on or off campus), including recruitment.

**Newly established fraternities/sororities/organizations** must register **immediately during the semester or trimester** of establishment.

Schools must promulgate registration guidelines within **60 days** of the Act's approval.

### Membership Lists

Upon registration, all fraternities/sororities/organizations shall submit a comprehensive list of members, **updated not later than 30 days from the start of every semester or trimester**.

### Penalties for Non-Registration

School officials may impose suspension on the head and officers who fail to register or update rosters. **Failure to comply results in cancellation of registration**.

## Section 7 — Faculty Adviser

Schools shall require all fraternities/sororities/organizations, as a condition for accreditation or registration, to submit the **name(s) of their faculty advisers** who must **not be members** of the fraternity/sorority/organization. A **written acceptance or consent** from the selected adviser must be included.

The faculty adviser shall:

- Be responsible for **monitoring activities**
- Be a duly recognized active member, in good standing, of the school's faculty

**Presumption:** In case of any violation of this Act, **the faculty adviser is presumed to have knowledge of and consented** to the commission of unlawful acts.

## Section 8 — Role of Educational Institutions

Schools shall exercise reasonable supervision *in loco parentis* over student conduct — the diligence prudent parents would employ when disciplining and protecting their children. Schools must take **proactive steps** to protect students from hazing dangers.

### Required Programs

Schools shall:

- Implement an **information dissemination campaign at the start of every semester or trimester** about consequences of conducting/participating in hazing
- Conduct an **orientation program** on membership in a fraternity/sorority/organization at the start of every semester or trimester
- Encourage fraternities/sororities/organizations to engage in undertakings that foster holistic personal growth and contribute to solving relevant societal issues

## Section 9 — Registration of Community-Based Organizations

All new and existing **community-based fraternities/sororities/organizations** (and local chapters) shall register with the **barangay, municipality, or city** where they are primarily based.

Upon registration, a comprehensive list of members and officers shall be submitted, **updated yearly from the date of registration**.

## Section 10 — Regulation of Initiation Rites for Community-Based Organizations

Only initiation rites that do not constitute hazing shall be allowed, provided:

**(a)** A **written application** to the *punong barangay* (or municipal/city mayor) **not later than seven (7) days prior** to the scheduled initiation date.

**(b)** Place and date of initiation rites and names of recruits/neophytes/applicants.

**(c)** Undertaking that no harm of any kind shall be committed.

**(d)** A **medical certificate** of the recruit/neophyte/applicant must be attached to ensure fitness to undergo initiation involving physical activity not falling under hazing.

**(e)** Initiation rites shall **not last more than three (3) days**.

**(f)** Names of incumbent officers and persons taking charge.

**(g)** Application is **under oath** with a declaration that it has been posted on the official bulletin board of the barangay/municipal/city hall and the organization's office.

**(h)** Posted from time of submission, removed three (3) days after conduct.

## Section 11 — Monitoring of Community-Based Initiation Rites

The *punong barangay* (or municipal/city mayor) must assign **at least two (2) officials** to be present during the initiation and document the entire proceedings. Same liability rules apply: no liability attaches **unless they failed to perform an overt act** to prevent or stop hazing.

## Section 12 — Nullity of Waiver and Consent

**Any form of approval, consent, or agreement** (written or otherwise, or express waiver of the right to object) made by a recruit/neophyte/applicant prior to an initiation rite that involves inflicting physical or psychological suffering shall be **void and without binding effect**.

**The defense of consent is NOT available** to persons prosecuted under this Act.

## Section 13 — Administrative Sanctions

Responsible officials of the school, uniformed learning institutions, AFP, or PNP **may impose appropriate administrative sanctions** on persons charged under this Act — **even before their conviction** — after due notice and summary hearing.

## Section 14 — Penalties

### (a) Death, Rape, Sodomy, or Mutilation Results
**Reclusion perpetua + fine of ₱3,000,000.00** for those who actually planned or participated.

### (b) Reclusion Perpetua + Fine of ₱2,000,000.00

Imposed on:

1. All persons who **actually planned or participated** in the conduct of hazing
2. All **officers of the fraternity/sorority/organization** actually present during the hazing
3. **The adviser** present when hazing acts were committed who failed to take action or report
4. **Former officers, nonresident members, or alumni** present during the hazing
   - *If a Bar member:* subject to disciplinary proceedings by the Supreme Court
   - *If a PRC professional:* subject to disciplinary proceedings by the concerned Professional Regulatory Board, including **suspension of at least 3 years or revocation of license**
   - **Reinstatement** subject to affidavits from at least 3 disinterested persons, good moral certifications, and Board approval
5. **Officers or members** who knowingly cooperated by inducing the victim to be present
6. **Members present** during hazing when intoxicated or under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs

### (c) Reclusion Temporal (Maximum) + Fine of ₱1,000,000.00

All persons present in the conduct of the hazing.

### (d) Reclusion Temporal + Fine of ₱1,000,000.00

Former officers, nonresident members, or alumni who perform any act to **hide, conceal, or otherwise hamper or obstruct any investigation** after commission of the prohibited acts. Same Bar/PRC disciplinary rules apply.

### (e) Prision Correccional (Minimum)

Any person who shall **intimidate, threaten, force, or employ vexation** against another for the purpose of recruitment in joining or promoting a fraternity/sorority/organization.

> **Persistent and repeated proposal/invitation** made to a person who has twice refused = **prima facie evidence of vexation**.

### (f) ₱1,000,000.00 Fine on the School

If the fraternity/sorority/organization filed a **written application that was approved by the school** and hazing occurred during the initiation rites, OR when no school representatives were present per Section 5.

**If hazing was committed in circumvention of the Act**, school officials must investigate *motu propio* and take an active role to ascertain facts, identify witnesses, determine disciplinary sanctions, and assist police authorities.

### Additional Liability Provisions

- **Owner/lessee of the place** where hazing is conducted shall be liable as **principal** under (a) or (b) when they had actual knowledge but failed to act or report.
- **Parents** (if hazing is held in their home as officers/members) shall be liable as **principals** under (a) or (b) when they had actual knowledge but failed to act or report.
- **School authorities, faculty members, and barangay/municipal/city officials** shall be liable as **accomplice** and administratively accountable if it can be shown that they allowed or consented to the conduct of hazing, or where there is actual knowledge but failed to act or report.
- **Presence of any person** during the hazing (even non-members) is **prima facie evidence of participation as a principal**, unless they prevented the commission or promptly reported.
- **Incumbent officers** shall be **jointly liable** with members who actually participated.
- **No mitigating circumstance** of "no intention to commit so grave a wrong" applies.
- This section applies to **presidents, managers, directors, or other responsible officers of businesses or corporations** engaged in hazing as a requirement for employment.
- A **conviction by final judgment** shall be reflected in the **scholastic record, personal record, or employment record** of the person convicted, regardless of when the judgment became final.

## Section 15 — Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR)

CHED, together with DepEd, DOJ, DILG, DSWD, AFP, PNP, and NYC, shall promulgate the IRR **within 90 days** from the effectivity of this Act.

## Section 16 — Separability Clause

If any provision is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the other parts remain valid and effective.

## Section 17 — Repealing Clause

RA 8049 and all other laws, decrees, executive orders, proclamations, rules or regulations inconsistent with this Act are amended or modified accordingly.

## Section 18 — Effectivity

This Act shall take effect **fifteen (15) days** after its publication in the Official Gazette or in at least two (2) national newspapers of general circulation.
