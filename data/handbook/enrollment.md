# Enrollment & Registration

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 2 (Academic Regulations)

## General Guidelines

1. A student's registration (enrollment) in the University is understood as an expression of willingness to abide by all rules and regulations. **Non-compliance due to lack of awareness of the handbook's provisions is not excusable.**
2. As Ateneans, students are expected to apply themselves fully in their academic endeavors and engage actively in co- and extra-curricular activities. They are encouraged to participate in one or more co-curricular activities. The University reserves the right to advise against participation in activities that disrupt academic learning or hinder personal growth. Students are expected to:
   - Optimize student services for spiritual formation, psycho-emotional, and social development (Campus Ministry Office, Guidance and Counseling Office, SACSI Office).
   - Attend institutional functions such as the Mass of the Holy Spirit (Red Mass), St. Ignatius Mass, and other special University activities.
   - Participate in programs organized by the Office of Student Affairs — Orientation Seminar, Reorientation for Graduating Seniors, Tuition Fee Increase Consultation, Magis Awards, AtFest Week, and unit-initiated activities.
3. Students must inform/update parents or guardians of their academic standing, attendance status, and consequences of failures and absences.
4. Students are expected to use communication and information technology responsibly.
5. Communication and IT equipment may not be used in classrooms without the teacher's explicit permission.
6. Students are required to keep their contact information at the Registrar's Office up to date.
7. Students are responsible for the behavior of persons or outsiders who come onto campus on their behalf.
8. Students who bring vehicles onto campus must observe all campus traffic and parking regulations (including displaying the pass and ensuring vehicles do not contribute to environmental pollution).
9. **Eating and drinking are NOT allowed** in classrooms, laboratories, audio-visual rooms, and the JTB Libraries.
10. All students share the responsibility of maintaining a **"drug-free" and "smoke-free"** campus.

## Academic Calendar

Classes are on a semester basis. The school year is divided into two semesters:

- **First semester:** August to December
- **Second semester:** January to May
- **Third term:** June to July (6 weeks; 45 class hours per 3-unit subject)

A semester consists of **18 weeks** and **54 class hours for a 3-unit subject**.

## Registration Process

Registration is the process of enrolling in courses before the start of each academic term.

### Step 1: Online Academic Consultation
Through the **MyAdZU Portal**, with the home department.

- Students select subjects based on their program prospectus.
- Students consult the Home Department regarding their chosen subjects.
- Department Chair or designated faculty verifies subjects based on student status (regular/irregular), then approves the consulted subjects.

### Step 2: Assessment of Tuition and Fees
By the **College Registrar**. Before assessing, the Registrar's Office checks for completion of clearance. All offices must be marked as cleared before students move to assessment. Students can view clearance status through the portal.

### Step 3: Payment of Tuition and Fees
At the **Finance Office**. Students must pay at least the **minimum down payment** for the current term to be validated. Students can check the portal to verify their validated status.

### Validation Requirements

Only **officially enrolled (validated)** students are eligible to:

- Participate in class activities
- Submit assignments
- Take exams
- Receive final grades for the semester or term

**Important:** Students have a maximum of **6 weeks after the start of classes** to complete validation. If not validated after 6 weeks, the student is removed from the class list and considered not enrolled.

## Academic Consultations

Academic consultations are conducted **a week before the enrollment period** to help students make informed decisions about their academic journey.

- Students must see the department chair/dean or assigned teacher to assess academic progress and deficiencies following the student's plan of study.
- Faculty advisors offer insights on prerequisites, workload management, and resources.
- Students may use this opportunity to discuss issues, challenges, and difficulties with subjects or program.

## Adding / Dropping a Subject

Adding/dropping is the process of modifying course load by enrolling in a new subject (adding) or withdrawing from a registered subject (dropping) within a specified period.

1. Dropping/adding is considered official only after completing a special **ADDING/DROPPING FORM** approved by the College Registrar.
2. The designated period is the **first two weeks of the regular semester** and the **first week of the third term**.
3. **Dropping a subject WITHOUT permission** from the University Registrar is recorded as **WF (Withdrawn with Failure)**.
4. A student may drop a subject and/or change to another subject (or section) **only if**:
   - The subject has been dissolved.
   - The schedule conflicts with another subject.
   - The subject conflicts with the student's work schedule (certification from employer must be presented to the Registrar).
   - Dropping is made during the designated schedule.

## Overload

Students are generally expected to enroll in the **maximum course load** specified in their official curriculum or program prospectus. However, under certain conditions, students may be permitted to enroll beyond this maximum **with prior approval from the Vice President for Higher Education (VPHE)**:

1. **Seniors and students in their final year** may take up to **six (6) additional units** beyond their maximum load for the entire school year.
2. Any exceptions require VPHE approval.

## Leave of Absence (LOA)

A **Leave of Absence (LOA)** is a period during which a student temporarily discontinues studies for **non-academic and non-disciplinary** reasons.

1. Students who do not plan to enroll in a specific semester or entire school year for non-academic/non-disciplinary reasons must apply for an LOA.
2. **LOA may NOT be granted** to students on academic probation or with unresolved disciplinary cases. Exceptions may be approved by the VPHE (academic matters) or by the Director of OSA (disciplinary concerns).
3. Students on LOA are **not permitted to study or enroll in another educational institution** during the leave period.
4. The duration of an LOA may **not exceed one academic year** and is allowed **only once** during the student's entire tenure at AdZU. The VPHE may grant exceptions.

## Withdrawal with Permission (WP)

1. **WP** is given to all subjects when enrollment is withdrawn within the first 6 weeks of the semester.
2. WP is also given to all subjects when enrollment is withdrawn anytime during the semester due to valid reasons (e.g., medical conditions, accidents, transfer of residence). Proper documents must be submitted as evidence.
3. WP is given to all subjects when the student withdraws from school.
4. The **University Refund Policy** applies to cases of Withdrawal with Permission.
5. WP is **NOT given selectively** to individual subjects — only to all subjects in a semester due to withdrawal from enrollment.
6. The student must apply for **LOA** (duly approved by the College Registrar) if intending to resume schooling in the future.
