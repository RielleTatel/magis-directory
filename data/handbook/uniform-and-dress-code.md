# Uniform & Dress Code

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 4 (Non-Academic Regulations) & Appendix A

Each student represents the University and is expected to wear proper attire when attending classes, visiting University premises, or participating in sanctioned activities elsewhere.

## Uniform Days

**Prescribed uniforms must be worn on MONDAY, TUESDAY, THURSDAY, AND FRIDAY.**

### For Male Students

a. **White short-sleeved polo** — AdZU seal on the left chest area
b. **White undershirt**
c. **Black slacks** (NOT denim or maong)
d. **Black shoes** (NOT sneakers or rubber shoes) worn with socks

### For Female Students

a. **White blouse** with broken-line stitches along the short sleeves, collar, and button area. AdZU seal on the left chest area. Dark blue buttons.
b. **Dark blue slacks** OR **A-shaped skirt** long enough to cover the knee (with an overlapping slit at the back, 4 inches above the knee)
c. **Black shoes** (not sandals or other open-toed footwear)

### For Female Muslim Students

a. **Plain dark blue hijab or headscarf** (matching the slacks; no prints or embellishments)
b. **White blouse** (long enough to cover the backside) with long sleeves up to the wrist; broken-line stitches along the edge of sleeves, collar, and button area; AdZU logo at the left side pocket; dark blue buttons
c. **Dark blue loose-fitting appropriate slacks**
d. **Black shoes**

**Hijab Convention:** Female Muslim students who wear a hijab should come to school following the conventions of the Islamic faith.

**Niqab Restriction:** For security reasons and proper identification, wearing a **niqab** or any garment/headdress covering the entire face is **NOT permitted**.

## Non-Uniform Days

**WEDNESDAY AND SATURDAY are designated as wash days.**

Students are expected to wear **gender-affirming apparel** based on their SOGIESC, subject to the prohibitions below.

> **SOGIESC** stands for Sexual Orientation, Gender Identity, gender Expression and Sex Characteristics.

## Prohibited Attire (Non-Uniform Days)

No student is allowed entry to campus or sanctioned off-campus activities wearing:

1. **See-through or semi-transparent clothing**
2. **Garments or accessories** (bags, mobile phone cases, etc.) with inappropriate, obscene, or vulgar designs
3. **Sleeveless shirts** with wide and loose arm holes, sandos, bicycle or athletic shorts (except when playing sports in the MPCC, gym, or backfield)
4. **Flip-flop slippers**
5. **Uniforms, shirts, or clothing with symbols/designs of Greek-lettered fraternities** or organizations espousing values counter to Filipino and Ignatian traditions
6. **Hats and caps** worn inside classrooms and offices
7. **Halter tops, spaghetti straps, and tube tops** worn without a cover-up (e.g., bolero, blazer, coat)
8. **Low-rider pants** (i.e., showing the buttocks)
9. Pants made of **Lycra spandex** or other stretch fabric without a cover-up (shorts, skirts)
10. Any attire considered **too revealing** (e.g., deep V-lines, short shorts, micro skirts)

## Dressing According to One's Identified Gender

For **non-uniform days**, permission to dress according to one's identified gender may be granted based on:

1. **Sex or Gender Transition**
2. **Gender identity and expression** — personal sense of identity and manner of clothing

### Procedure & Requirements

Submit an application to the **OSA** with:

a. Accomplished **Student's Declaration & Conforme Form**
b. **Letter of intent** outlining pertinent reasons, signed by the student
c. **Photocopy of Matriculation Form and School ID**
d. Additional documents as required by OSA (e.g., proof of independence from parents/guardians)

### Process

1. The **Director of Student Affairs** verifies completeness/authenticity and initially assesses validity. Additional documents or meetings with the student or parent may be requested.
2. The **final decision** is made after submitted documents have been verified.
3. Upon approval, OSA informs professors through their AdZU mail for proper dissemination.

The consent is effective from the date of approval until the duration of the student's college stay. **Students with permission must still follow the College Uniform and Dress Code, including policies for non-uniform days.**

## Exemption from Wearing Uniform

The following are exempted from wearing the prescribed uniform:

1. **Married students who are pregnant**
2. **Working students** required to wear office uniforms
3. **Any other reason** determined by OSA (e.g., students in mourning, medical reasons, physical disability, force majeure such as fire or flood) — provided they obtain **exemption slips from OSA** and attend classes in decent attire.

## PATHFIT and Practicum Uniforms

- **PATHFIT uniforms** should be worn **ONLY in PATHFIT classes** or PATHFIT-related activities. Exemptions may be granted if the next class is scheduled shortly afterward (limited time to change clothes).
- Students on **RLE, internship, or practicum** are permitted to wear their proper uniforms as required by their respective department or college.

## Violation Slip Procedure

1. The **Information Desk-assigned guard from Gate 2** and **assigned guards from Gate 6** are authorized to issue violation slips and assess non-compliance (e.g., dress code, ID issues).
2. Students must **fill out the violation slip**; the guard records it after assessment.
3. **Two copies** are made — one for OSA, one for the student.
4. The student must **visit the OSA** to present their violation slip. OSA reviews records to determine the number of violations and appropriate sanctions.
5. If the student fails to visit OSA, the Information Desk's copy is still submitted to OSA for monitoring and compliance.

## Haircut

All students must maintain a **decent haircut**.

## Identification Card (ID)

1. The **Office of Student Affairs** issues ID cards to all students for identification, protection, and security.
2. Students must wear/present the ID to the security guard on duty **before entering the campus**.
3. **No student will be allowed entry** at any time on any day (including Sundays and holidays) unless they present an authorization approved by the **VP for Administration**.
4. Students must **visibly display their ID at all times**, both on and off campus.
5. The ID must be **properly pinned on the chest area** of the uniform/shirt, or hung with an ID lace.
6. **Borrowing, lending, and using somebody else's ID is strictly prohibited.** Tampering with data or altering the photo renders the ID invalid. Manufacturing or using fake IDs is likewise prohibited.
7. **Loss or theft of an ID** must be reported immediately to OSA. Upon presentation of a **notarized affidavit of loss** and **official receipt of payment** for a new ID card.
8. Students must report (in writing) any change in personal information (residence, civil status) to OSA and the Registrar's Office as early as possible. Letters sent to addresses on record at OSA are considered officially received.

## Repeated Violations

| Situation     | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| 1st Offense   | Oral Reprimand |
| 2nd Offense   | Written Reprimand and Response + 3-hour Community Service |
| 3rd Offense   | 2-day Suspension |
