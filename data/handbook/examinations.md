# Examinations

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 2 (Academic Regulations)

## Examination Slips (Admit-to-Exam)

Students are required to present their **examination slips** to the proctors as proof of financial settlement before being allowed to take the **Midterm and Final examinations**.

- Failure to present a valid examination slip results in **ineligibility to participate in the exams**.
- Students must settle all outstanding tuition and fees at the **University Finance Office** before the examination period.
- The University strongly encourages students and parents to complete all financial obligations early to prevent inconvenience or stress during examinations.

## Midterm and Final Examinations

1. The schedule of Midterm and Final Examinations is posted on the **Registrar's bulletin board** and online at the university's website (**MyAdZU Portal**).
2. Students can only take these examinations when they present their **"Admit-to-Exam" slips**, secured from the **Finance Office**.
3. Students must:
   - Display their **Identification Cards** properly
   - Adhere to the **university's dress code**
   - Ensure all **electronic devices are turned off**
4. Students who miss the midterm or final examinations should inform their respective teachers immediately so that proper assessment and action can be taken.
5. A **special delayed examination** may be given due to:
   - Illness
   - Family bereavement
   - Emergency circumstances

   Pertinent documents (hospital records, medical certificates, etc.) must support such reasons.
6. **Delayed examinations** must be taken within **two (2) weeks** immediately after the last day of midterm or final exams.
7. Students may request to see their corrected examination papers and the computation of their final grade. If errors are discovered, a student may appeal for a grade change within the prescribed period.
8. **All student activities are suspended one week before and during the midterm and final examinations** to help students focus on academic preparations.

## Grade When No Final Exam Is Taken

1. **Failure to take the delayed examination** for reasons NOT considered valid merits a percent correct of **0.0**.
2. A student who is unable to take the final examination for **valid reasons** may be given a final grade by computing the average of the **first and second quarters** of the semester.

If the teacher concerned is unable to do so, the **department chair** or the **dean of the college** may decide on the status of the student's final grade.

## Exemptions from Final Examinations

Exemption from taking the final examination is a **privilege** that may be granted to high-performing students. The following conditions must be met:

1. The **department** that offers or oversees the course or subject **allows the granting of exemption**.
2. When there is no contradictory policy, the **faculty member handling the course has the prerogative** to grant an exemption.
3. In the **second quarter**, the student must have obtained:
   - **Midterm grade of 4.00**, AND
   - **Class standing grade of 4.00**

**No exemptions are given for board courses.**

## Student Rights Regarding Grades

Students are entitled to:

- See their **graded quizzes and other requirements**.
- Be informed by their teachers of their **class standing, midterm and final grades, and the computations**.
