# Student Services

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 5 (Student Services)

AdZU offers a wide range of student services to support holistic formation. This document covers all offices and their programs.

---

## A. Office of Admissions and Aid (OAA)

**Vision.** A service-oriented office where the atmosphere is one in which all can live and work together in understanding and love, with respect for all men and women as children of God. Personal care to students, parents, faculty, staff, and visitors is demonstrated through friendly, prompt, and courteous service.

**Mission.**

- To uphold the dignity of the human person by offering quality service to all persons regardless of race, ethnic background, religion, color, national ancestry, sex, age, disability, and employment status.
- To foster the Ignatian values of **Magis** and **Cura Personalis** in the life of the OAA and the Ateneo community.
- To assist the University in providing more educational opportunities to those who need them and form scholars who will become future leaders of the country.

**Objectives:**

- Plan and carry out an effective **Recruitment Campaign** for new students and inform them of available scholarship opportunities.
- Cooperate with the colleges by implementing admissions policies, marketing strategies, and fundraising opportunities to generate more funds for scholarships.
- Carry out registration procedures for first-year and new students.
- Provide pertinent information to all inquiries (telephone and personal).

**Services/Programs Offered:**

- College Freshmen Enrollment
- Shifters' evaluation of grades and College Entrance Test results
- Transferees Enrollment
- Recruitment campaign
- **College Entrance Test (CET)** Administration
- Processing of **Scholarship** application requirements, interviews & home visits
- **Ateneo Senior Summer Program**

Admission to AdZU is determined by high school grades, an interview for select programs, and the CET score. Different schools/colleges have their own admission and retention policies, particularly for programs requiring licensure exams.

## B. College Registrar's Office (CRO)

The CRO oversees course registration, maintains student records, manages graduation procedures, and addresses curricular and academic matters.

### Admission Functions

- Work with the admission office and colleges to manage admission requirements, transfer credits, and student retention.
- Evaluate and check student requirements: **Form 137**, **birth certificate**, and **student information sheet**.

### Registration Functions

- Collaborate with finance and colleges to manage consultation, assessment, and validation.
- Prepare reports related to enrollment — summary enrollment reports for the VPHE office; enrollment data (projected vs. actual) for the Board of Trustees; freshmen and transferee reports for OAA; foreign students report for CHED.

### Records Management

- Oversee student record management, graduation, and associated services, ensuring information is updated and accurate.
- Implement procedures to maintain the integrity, safety, and confidentiality of files and records.
- Work with the VPHE office and colleges to update the enrollment system with new/revised prospectuses.
- Ensure compliance with academic regulations — timely submission of grades, monitoring incomplete grades, preparing the **Dean's List**.
- Uphold graduation requirements; compile, verify, and organize the roster of eligible candidates for graduation.
- Update CHED on annual data collection through the **Higher Education Management Information System (HEMIS)**.
- Direct reconstruction of lost or missing records.

### Clerical / Administrative Tasks

- Prepare course schedules for three semesters (1st, 2nd, 3rd terms) and exam schedules for midterms and finals.
- Prepare the **school calendar** for each academic year.
- Submit list of graduates to **CHED IX**; submit list of graduates eligible for board exams to the **Professional Regulation Commission (PRC)**.
- Receive, process, and dispatch records or transcripts for active and inactive students.
- Issue certificates, grades, records, release transfer credentials, and diplomas.
- Attend to transactions with government offices/agencies.
- Resolve questions regarding academic policies/regulations related to student records.
- Provide statistical data on enrollment.
- Maintain linkages with other institutional registrars.

### Recollection
A one-day off-campus, non-academic activity. Each student is required to attend one recollection in their **2nd and 3rd years** of college. ## D. College Guidance and Counseling Office (CGCO)

The CGCO works collaboratively with students, academic units, faculty, student services, and other departments to enhance students' **academic, personal–social, and career success**.

- Counseling services are available to all enrolled students.
- Upon enrollment, students must fill out the **Student Information Form (SIF)** — the Guidance Form — which becomes the basis for student records with CGCO.
- Supported by the **Guidance Fee** paid each semester (part of miscellaneous fees).
- A **testing fee** for the psychological test is collected for all first-year students upon enrollment during the first semester. Test administration is scheduled during **FFP classes**.
- Test results are interpreted individually by a guidance counselor (by appointment).
- Alumni, faculty, and staff may consult with CGCO counselors (subject to special arrangements; students take priority). Counseling sessions for external clients are usually on Saturdays, 1:00–5:00 pm. A **professional fee** is collected from alumni and external clients.

### Programs and Services

1. **Counseling** — Professional relationship to help individuals/families/groups accomplish mental health, wellness, education, and career goals. Strict confidentiality maintained.
2. **Entry Interview** — For all first-year students and transferees. Builds rapport and identifies adjustment/transition concerns.
3. **Individual Counseling** — One-on-one sessions for solutions to doubts and challenges.
4. **Group Counseling** — Two or more students with similar concerns.
5. **Psychological First Aid** — Immediate intervention for clients with post-traumatic stress. Available for internal and external clients (outreach during crises).
6. **Student Group Guidance** — Program called **Adjustment 101**, designed for first-year students to help them transition from Senior High School to College. Coordinated with FFP.
7. **Workshops (on request):**
   - Assertiveness Training
   - Emotion Regulation
   - Interpersonal Relationships
   - Motivation Building
   - Personality Development
   - Stress Management
   - Time Management
   - Others
8. **Career Development Program** (for 1st–4th year students):
   - **Freshmen Formation Program (FFP)** — career session integrated into the module.
   - **Know Now Career Awareness Lecture-Workshop** — for Sophomores.
   - **Career Builder Lecture-Workshop** — for Juniors. Topics: Resume Writing, Cover Letters, Job Interviews (tips/pointers), Job Hunting Strategies, Power Dressing.
   - **Career Builder Refresher Series** — for Seniors.
   - **Career Fair** — 2-day culminating activity. Some companies hire on the spot. Alumni invited.
   - **Company Career Talks** — offered by request/invitation.
9. **Academic Resiliency Program** — Assists students whose WPA does not meet retention policy. Support through Individual Consultation/Counseling and Workshops on study skills, motivation, and other themes.
10. **Peers' Circle** — Formation program for students with a spirit of service:
    - **General Assemblies** — discuss group matters.
    - **Tutorial Services** — peer support for academic challenges.
    - **Round Table Discussions** — monthly student-wellness topics.
    - **Inspirational Bowl** — every Wednesday at Paseo de Maria catwalk.
    - **Kumustahan Sessions** — last Friday of each quarter.
    - **Consultation** — peers consult counselors on personal matters.
11. **Student Testing** — All first-year students must take the **College Adjustment Scales (CAS)** with one-on-one interpretation by a counselor.
12. **Student Inventory** — Files updated annually, kept confidential, used for shifting/transfer recommendations, case conferences, and employment recommendations. Files kept in archive **until five years after graduation**, then disposed of responsibly.
13. **Services for Faculty and Staff:**
    - **Consultation** — for the AdZU community on programs/services and personal/professional concerns.
    - **Individual counseling** — may be recommended; confidentiality maintained.
14. **Special Services:**
    - Facilitation in research data identification
    - Psychological First Aid
    - Counseling and Psychology Internship Program
    - Information Service
    - Referral Resources
    - Counselor Training and Sessions

### General Library Regulations

a. Observe **silence**. Idle conversation, loud laughter, and unnecessary noise disturb clients. Subdued tone only.
b. **No eating, smoking, or sleeping** in the library.
c. Books in reading areas should **NOT be returned to shelves** — leave on tables. Exception: encyclopedia volumes return to their proper place.
d. Push chair back against the table before leaving.
e. Students have unrestricted access to **Reference, Filipiniana, American Corner, and General Collection** shelves. **Graduate/Faculty Section** is restricted to faculty, Special Program in Nursing students, and graduate students. Undergraduates may ask permission from the librarian.

### Non-Loaning Materials (read-only)

- General References (encyclopedias, dictionaries, atlases, almanacs, yearbooks, etc.)
- Art books
- Special Collections
- Theses/Dissertations
- Periodicals
- Non-print materials
- Electronic materials

### Control Section Procedure

The library does NOT have a theft detection system. Instead, guards monitor the Control Section.

- Library users must **wear IDs at all times** when inside the library.
- Upon leaving the library, every user must:
  - Open and show contents of bags, laptop bags, big envelopes, folders, etc.
  - Show the due date slip for every library material taken out.

### Loaning Procedures & Policies

**Requirements for New Library Users:**

- Present validated enrollment form
- Fill out library registration form
- Submit recent ID pictures (first-year college students)
- Activate library records every semester (including summer) to borrow books (for old students)

**Borrowing Procedure:**

- Search book(s) on **OPAC** and locate on shelves.
- Present school ID at the **Circulation Desk** with the book(s).
- Allow the Circulation Librarian to verify eligibility. If eligible, librarian checks out books.
- Librarian stamps the due date on the back of book(s).
- Wait for the transaction receipt.
- Sign the transaction receipt (librarian keeps on file).
- Present borrowed library materials at the Control Section for inspection upon leaving.

### Loan Limits

| Patron Type        | Number of Books | Duration    |
|--------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| College Students   | 5               | 7 days      |
| Medical Students   | 3               | 7 days      |
| Graduate Students  | 5               | 2 weeks     |
| Staff              | 5               | 2 weeks     |
| PT Faculty         | 5–7             | 1 semester  |
| FT Faculty         | 10–15           | 1 semester  |

**Returning:**

a. Return books to the Circulation Desk for overdue check.
b. Pay overdue fines and present receipt to clear the account.
c. Sign transaction receipt and return to librarian.

**Renewing:**

a. Bring borrowed books to Circulation Desk.
b. Books may be renewed **twice** if not in high demand.
c. **Telephone renewal NOT accepted.**

**Reserving:**

- Faculty may reserve books for course assignments. Books may only be read within the library unless professor requests limited circulation.
- Students may borrow reserve books on an **hourly basis**, renewable for another hour if no requests.
- Books for **overnight use** are available from **4:00 PM to 11:00 AM, Monday–Friday**. Saturday borrowings must be returned **8:00 AM the next service day**.

**Holds:** Registered users may place a hold on an item that is checked out. Notification is sent when the item is returned.

**Borrower Confidentiality:** Circulation records are not disclosed (which materials are checked out, by whom). For urgent needs, staff will attempt to contact the borrower.

### Fines and Penalties

| Material Type | Fine |
|--------------|------|
| General collection, American Corner, Filipiniana, Fiction | **₱5.00 per day** |
| Reserved books | **₱1.00 per hour**; equivalent to ₱5.00 per full day (including Saturday if service day) |

- Overdue fines of **₱10.00 or less** are paid at the **Circulation Desk**.
- Fines **above ₱10.00** are paid at the **Finance Office**.
- Receipt must be presented to the Circulation Librarian for clearance.
- Delinquent borrowers are reminded; names posted at the Circulation bulletin board twice a month.
- Students cannot borrow books unless their library accounts are settled and all borrowed books are returned.

### Lost Books

a. Report lost books **immediately**. Overdue fines continue to accumulate until notification.
b. Lost books must be **replaced** + accumulated fines + **₱100.00 processing fee**. (Faculty: no overdue fines.)
c. Replacement must be the **same title, edition, and author**. If not possible, a book on the same/closely related subject — **most recent edition**, priced at the same level or higher.
d. If the book is no longer available, the **library director** decides on a replacement.
e. If the patron finds the book after declaring it lost and returns it in acceptable condition, only accumulated overdue fines must be paid.

### Mutilation and Stealing of Library Materials

**Serious offenses** punishable by a **fine equivalent to the cost of replacing/restoring** the material. Depending on severity, additional penalties may include **expulsion or suspension**.

## F. University Infirmary

**Vision.** The University Infirmary envisions itself as the catalyst for healthcare and services, advancing the well-being of the AdZU community and beyond.

**Mission.** Progressing the health of the AdZU community without compromise through **SERVICE** and the provision of a healthful environment toward health promotion and disease prevention through early detection and follow-up.

**Core Values:**

1. **Compassion** — Treat clients (students, faculty, staff, Jesuit community) with kindness and empathy.
2. **Respect** — Honor rights to privacy and confidentiality; value differences.
3. **Accountability** — Be responsible for work and client outcomes daily.
4. **Excellence** — Strive to be the best; continuously improve.
5. **Integrity** — Do the right things at the right time, focusing on the interest of every student, faculty, staff, and Jesuit community member.

### Services

1. Annual Medical-Dental Clearance
2. Free Medical-Dental Consultation / Teleconsulting
3. Free over-the-counter medicines
4. Medical Certificate for out-of-campus activities
5. Stress Test
6. Blood Pressure Monitoring
7. Hemoglucotest (Sugar)
8. Electrocardiogram
9. Medical First Aid treatment
10. Health Counseling
11. Assistance in facilitating Insurance claims
12. Additional Dental Services with minimal fees:
    - Dental Prophylaxis
    - Tooth Filling
    - Tooth Extraction

### Programs

#### 1. Mental Health and Wellness Programs

Students must undergo a medical clearance process to qualify. Facilities used: **Sauras Fitness Center** (top floor of Sauras Building), **Multi-Purpose Covered Courts 1 and 2**, **AdZU Backfield**, **Lantaka Pool**.

a. **Weight Reduction/Training Program** — Easy-to-learn exercise setups for weight targets.
b. **Dance Fitness Program** — Stress relief through dance.
c. **Yoga** — Calming routine; gentler approach to exercise.
d. **Muay Thai** — Martial arts training with expert trainers.

#### 2. Health Awareness and Prevention Programs

Supports the CHED mandate to make programs available for students.

a. **HIV-AIDS Prevention**

c. **Hepatitis B** — 12–16% of Philippine population infected; AdZU educates and guides prevention.
d. **Pulmonary Tuberculosis** — TB is curable with proper drugs and early detection.

#### 3. Health-Help Desk

Facilitates **referrals** for students needing further evaluation/management through AdZU's extensive network of experts and specialists.
