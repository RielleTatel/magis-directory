# Emergency Evacuation Procedures

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Appendix C

All members of the Ateneo de Zamboanga University community should be familiar with the **Emergency Evacuation procedure**. All must know the location of exits and designated assembly areas. Evacuation follows building evacuation routes illustrated on posters located in strategic places throughout campus.

## General Evacuation Procedure

1. Upon hearing the **warning signal (siren)**, immediately cease all activities and secure personal valuables.

   - The **AdZU Disaster Risk and Reduction Management Team (UDRRM)** and **University Security Office (USO)** personnel assess the situation.
   - The Team disseminates information on the situation to all key administrators (President, VPHE, Unit Heads, Deans, etc.).
   - Simultaneously, the USO initiates a **LOCKDOWN OF THE CAMPUS** through Security Guards on Duty.
   - During lockdowns, **all entry and exit points are blocked**. Faculty, staff, and students are only allowed to leave after the **incident commander gives the all-clear signal**.

2. Move **quickly but calmly** from one location to the designated assembly areas. Bring all personal items. Follow Team Leaders' directions strictly.

3. Office heads, staff, and faculty members ensure that everyone in offices and classrooms has vacated their areas.

4. Before leaving the building, **close the doors** (but do not lock). Take the nearest marked exit leading to the designated Assembly Area. Alert others to do the same.

5. **Persons with disabilities (PWDs) and senior citizens** should be assisted in exiting the building.

6. **At the assembly area**, wait for instructions from a University official.
   - The University Infirmary provides medical services.
   - Faculty members instruct students to **contact parents/guardians immediately** to inform them of the situation and ask to be picked up. Parents/guardians must bring a **valid ID** to present to the guard.

7. The **University Communications Office** handles press releases and media announcements.

## Reminders

- Two (2) persons will make sure doors are open during the earthquake drill. They shall execute the **duck, cover, and hold posture** by the door.
- Main concern during an earthquake is to protect oneself:
  - Execute **duck, cover, and hold posture**
  - Take cover under a **sturdy table or doorway**
  - Watch out for **falling objects**
  - **Keep calm**

## In Case of Earthquake

### Pre-Earthquake

1. Identify **Safe Spots** in classrooms and offices (tables, desks, etc.).
2. Identify **danger zones** — windows with glass, bookshelves, machinery, cabinets, furniture that may topple or slide, hanging and heavy objects.

### Phases of an Earthquake Drill

#### Phase 1: ALARM
- A siren signals an earthquake.
- The siren is the signal for all to execute the **DUCK, COVER, and HOLD** posture.
- In an **actual earthquake**, the shaking itself is the signal.

#### Phase 2: RESPONSE
- While the siren sounds, move away from windows, glass, or light fixtures.
- Assume **"duck, cover, and hold"** posture under desks, tables, chairs, or safe spots.
- **Remain in position until shaking stops.**
- During an actual earthquake, OSA personnel will sound the siren after initial shaking.
- The **Incident Commander**, through the PA system, will inform the community and give instructions.

#### Phase 3: EVACUATION
- Once shaking stops, evacuate the building to the evacuation areas through predetermined routes/exits.
- Proceed **quickly and calmly** to the nearest exit. Emergency leaders direct you. **Do not panic.**
- Classrooms, laboratories, library, and offices should be **left unlocked** when leaving.
- **Ideally, students leave in twos.**

#### Phase 4: ASSEMBLY

**Evacuation Procedure Map:**

```
        WARNING BELL/ALARM
        Siren (One Long Ring)
                ↓
        STOP ALL ACTIVITIES
        (Classes, programs, meetings)
                ↓
        EXIT TO ASSEMBLY AREA
                ↓
        "ALL CLEAR" SIGNAL
        Siren (One Long Ring)
```

**STAY IN ASSEMBLY AREA UNTIL "ALL CLEAR" SIGNAL IS GIVEN.**

At the evacuation area, students should **stay with classmates** or other students from the same class. Staff and office personnel should remain in one group.

#### Phase 5: HEAD COUNT
- Teachers do a head count and report to Team Leaders.
- Team Leaders report to Evacuation Area Facilitators.
- Faculty instructs students to **contact parents/guardians** with a valid ID.
- The University Communications Office handles press releases and media.

#### Phase 6: EVALUATION
An evaluation of the drill is conducted to identify problems and propose solutions for future drills.

## In Case of Fire

1. A **siren** will sound to signal a halt to all work and classes.
2. As soon as the alarm sounds, all shall **evacuate the building**.
3. Students must exit the classroom in an orderly manner through the nearest stairway or exit. (Be familiar with the building layout. Exit routes are posted in accessible areas.)

### Fire Safety Rules

- Team Leaders guide students to the nearest assembly areas.
- If you are the **last person to exit** a classroom or office, close the door but **do not lock**.
- **If your clothes catch fire — STOP, DROP, AND ROLL.**
- Teachers must ensure all students have left the classroom.
- Everyone must remain in the assembly area until the **all-clear signal** is sounded (manual siren).

## In Case of Injury

1. **Stay calm.**
2. Send somebody to **inform the school nurse** about the incident.
3. Send somebody to **inform the OSA**.
4. **Do NOT move the victim** unless extremely necessary to avoid further injury.
5. Keep the victim comfortable and calm until help arrives.

---

## In Case of Bomb Threat / Blast

1. The **University Security Office assesses** the situation and recommends the proper course of action to the VPHE and the President.
2. **KEEP CALM AND DO NOT PANIC.** Teachers must instruct students to remain seated inside the classroom. No one stands up.
3. **Check immediate surroundings** (trash cans, chairs, tables, corridors) for suspicious-looking packages or bags. If none found, stay where you are.
4. If suspicious-looking packages/bags are found, **DO NOT TOUCH THEM**. Send the **beadle/student representative** to the OSA or security guard on duty to relay the information.
5. Instruct students to leave the classroom immediately in an **orderly manner**, following evacuation procedures.
6. Proceed to assembly points. Once in the evacuation/safe area, conduct a **head count** of all faculty, staff, and students.
7. Students, faculty, and staff must remain in the assembly area until the **all-clear signal** is given (manual siren).
