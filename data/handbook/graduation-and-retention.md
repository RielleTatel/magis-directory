# Graduation, Clearance & Retention

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 2 (Academic Regulations)

## Ateneo Diploma Eligibility

To be eligible for an Ateneo de Zamboanga University diploma, a candidate for graduation should:

- Complete a **minimum weighted Grade Point Average (GPA) of 1.00** in the required subjects of the degree program.
- Maintain a residency at the University of **at least three (3) years** or **six (6) semesters** plus required third-term sessions — equivalently, the student must have taken **at least 75% of the total required units** at the University.

## Student Clearance

The clearance process ensures that students have fulfilled their **academic, financial, and administrative obligations** before progressing to the next academic level or graduating.

### Clearance Requirements for Moving Up

**a. Eligibility / Academic:**

- Submission of all academic assignments and projects
- Completion of required examinations and assessments
- Fulfillment of attendance requirements

**b. Financial Obligations:**
All tuition fees, miscellaneous fees, and penalties (if applicable) must be settled in full.

**c. Library and Laboratory Accounts:**
Return all borrowed books, materials, and equipment in good condition. Outstanding fines or charges must be settled.

**d. Administrative Compliance:**
Compliance with institutional policies, including:

- Evaluating faculty through the MyAdZU Portal
- Submitting required documents — Form 137, Birth Certificate, Certificate of Good Morals, updated personal information forms, or parental consent forms (if applicable)

### Clearance Requirements for Graduation

**a. Academic Completion:**
Satisfy ALL academic requirements of the program, including internships, theses, or capstone projects.

**b. Financial Obligations:**
No outstanding balances with the institution — tuition, fines, or other charges.

**c. Library and Laboratory Accounts:**
All borrowed materials/equipment returned; any outstanding fines paid.

**d. Graduation Application and Exit Interview:**
Submit a completed **graduation application form** at the beginning of the final semester. Participate in an **exit interview or survey** as part of the institutional feedback process.

### Clearance Process

- **Access:** Clearance is accessible (view-only) to students through the **MyAdZU Portal**.
- **Verification and Approval:** Offices can see and update the status through the **AdZU College Clearance System** (aps.AdZU.edu.ph).
- **Deadlines:**
  - **Graduating students** must complete clearances **immediately after their final exams**.
  - **Other students** are highly encouraged to fulfill clearance requirements **a week before enrollment**, so they are already cleared upon assessment. Late submissions may delay advancement to the next level or graduation.

### Consequences of Non-Compliance

Students who fail to complete the clearance process will:

1. Be **ineligible to enroll** in the next academic level.
2. Be **barred from participating in graduation ceremonies**.
3. Be **unable to obtain official documents** such as diplomas, transcripts, or certificates of graduation.

Additional penalties may be imposed following AdZU policies.

## Retention / Continuance in the University

All students must meet the **minimum grade requirement** and the **weighted grade average (GPA)** required in their program to graduate with a bachelor's degree.

- All students are expected to maintain a **GPA of at least 1.0 every semester** to remain in good academic standing.
- Students are responsible for monitoring their final grades, GPA, and whether they have met the minimum requirement every semester (available in the MyAdZU Portal).
- For **Board programs** or programs whose graduates take licensure exams, retention policies are **specific to the program**. The department concerned provides a comprehensive orientation to new first-year students, who are required to sign a consent letter indicating they understand and will abide by the program's policies.

## Academic Probation

A student who fails to maintain the required GPA is placed on **academic probation**. A student on probation who fails to achieve the required GPA receives a **warning status** and is subsequently dismissed with a **conditionally discharged status**.

### Probation Tiers

**a. Academic Probation Status**
- Maximum enrollment: **18 units**
- End-of-semester GPA requirement: **at least 1.0**
- If met → reinstated to regular academic status
- If not met → Warning Status

**b. Warning Status**
- Maximum enrollment: **15 units**
- End-of-semester GPA requirement: **at least 1.0**
- If met → reinstated to Probation Status
- If not met → Conditionally Discharged Status

**c. Conditionally Discharged Status**
- Student is **enrolled at another school for at least 15 units** in a semester.
- If the student earns a GPA of **at least 2.5** (or equivalent) at the other school, they may enroll back at AdZU with **Probationary Status**.

### Enrollment Procedure for Students on Probation Status

1. **Department Evaluation** — Student goes to the department; chair tags student as evaluated in the Probation Monitoring System and prints two (2) copies of the probation form for the parent/guardian's signature.
2. **Guidance and Counseling Scheduling** — Student visits **CGCO** to schedule a counseling session. The guidance counselor sets up the Probation Monitoring System.
3. **Department Consultation** — Student returns to the department with two signed probation forms and parents/guardians. The chair explains implications, determines subjects (max 18 units) through **ACES (Ateneo Consultation and Evaluation System)**, and collects one signed form.
4. **Outcome** — At semester end, if GPA ≥ 1.0 → follow general enrollment procedure. If not → Warning Status.

### Enrollment Procedure for Students on Warning Status

1. **Department Evaluation** — Same process; probation form printed for parent/guardian signature.
2. **Guidance and Counseling Scheduling** — Student visits CGCO; counselor sets up Probation Monitoring System.
3. **College Consultation** — Student (with parent/guardian) proceeds to the **Dean** for consultation. The Dean explains the implications of Warning Status.
4. **Department Consultation** — Subjects determined (max **15 units**) through ACES.
5. **Outcome** — At semester end, if GPA ≥ 1.0 → return to Probation Status. If not → Conditionally Discharged Status (cannot enroll at AdZU the following semester).

### Conditionally Discharged Status Re-Entry

If the student obtained a GPA of at least 2.5 (or equivalent) in the latest semester (minimum 15 units) at another school and decides to re-enroll at AdZU, they follow the **returnee enrollment procedure** and proceed under the **Probation Status** procedure.

### Academic Standards Committee

The Academic Standards Committee hears and makes recommendations on student appeals from Conditionally Discharged Status, made through the VPHE for continuance of study.

**Composition:**

- Assistant to the VPHE (presiding officer)
- College Dean
- Department Chair
- Faculty who had the student in class
- Guidance Counselor (academic probation cases)
- Student Representative (**El Consejo**)

**Procedure for Appeal:**
The student writes a **formal letter of appeal** to the Assistant to the Vice President for Higher Education **two (2) weeks before the start of the regular enrollment period**. The letter must be countersigned by the parent or legal guardian and submitted along with pertinent supporting documents (e.g., medical certificate, marriage contract).

The committee's recommendations are sent to the **VPHE for approval by the President**.
