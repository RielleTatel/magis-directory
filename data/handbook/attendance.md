# Attendance & Classroom Conduct

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 2 (Academic Regulations)

## Students' Attendance

1. Students are expected to attend classes **promptly and regularly**.
2. A **class beadle** assists the teacher in monitoring attendance.
3. A student is **tardy (late)** when they come to class **after the opening prayer**.
4. **Two occasions of tardiness are counted as one absence.** Tardiness, however, should not bar any student from attending class.
5. There is a limit to the number of absences without academic sanction. A student is allowed the following number of hours of absence based on subject units:

| Subject Units | Maximum Allowable Absence (hours) |
|---------------|-----------------------------------|
| 2-unit subject | 5 hours                          |
| 3-unit subject | 7 hours                          |
| 5-unit subject | 12 hours                         |
| 6-unit subject | 14 hours                         |

6. Unless explicitly excused, a student who exceeds the allowed number of hours of absence is **dropped from the class roll** with a grade of **WF (Withdrawn with Failure)**.

7. **Absences may be excused** for the following reasons:
   - Hospitalization or illness (confirmed by a **medical certificate**).
   - **Demise of an immediate family member** (parent, brother/sister, spouse, or children).

   A student who is absent is responsible for all work assigned by the instructor during the absence.

8. The **subject teacher has discretion** to approve excusable absences. Students may appeal to the teachers concerned for consideration.

9. The faculty submits the official list of students "Dropped" due to excessive absences to the Office of Student Affairs. A summary is posted on the **College Bulletin Board**.

10. Students are expected to follow classroom policies and procedures set by the school and their teachers. A teacher may refer a student to the **OSA** for misbehavior to ensure proper disciplinary action.

11. The **Office of Student Affairs (OSA)** maintains and keeps records of student-related documents.

## Bell System

1. Classes begin and end with the **ringing of the bell**.
2. A **ten-minute interval** is observed between class periods.
3. **During emergencies, one long siren** will be sounded. In such instances, students should immediately cease all activities, vacate the building calmly, and quickly assemble at the designated evacuation areas.

## Opening and Closing Prayer

As a tradition in a Catholic Jesuit institution, **classes should commence and end with a prayer**. Students may lead the prayer voluntarily. Alternatively, the teacher may assign or request a student to lead the prayer.

## Make-up Classes

1. Make-up classes must have the **expressed approval of the Dean** or the **Vice President for Higher Education**.
2. Students must be informed **at least two (2) days before** the scheduled special class.
3. A special class schedule must be posted on the **OSA Bulletin Board**.
4. Make-up classes are allowed only if **at least 2/3 of the students agree** to the proposed make-up class.
5. Students must affix their signature on the sign-up sheet indicating agreement. The sheet must include the date, time, and venue, and must be attached to the request form/letter.
6. Any cancellation must be posted on the OSA Bulletin Board **at least one day before** the scheduled make-up class.

## Spiritual Formation (Required Attendance)

AdZU offers the following spiritual formation activities that students are required to attend:

1. A scheduled **one-day recollection** during the student's freshman, sophomore, and junior years.
2. A **three-day retreat** during the student's senior (or final) year.
3. **Muslim students and those of other religious affiliations** are provided with a separate schedule and a spiritual program tailored to their respective faith traditions.
4. Students (including irregular students) who attend their scheduled retreat or recollection are **officially excused from classes, Related Learning Experiences (RLE), examinations, and other academic requirements** during the specified day(s).
5. **Completing all assigned recollections and the senior retreat is REQUIRED for graduation.**
