# Student Clubs and Organizations

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 6 (Campus Clubs and Student Organizations)

## Authority and Governance

### A. Authority to Operate
The **Director of Student Affairs (DSA)** regulates the operation of student organizations. The DSA may recommend to the **VPHE** the cancellation of the authority to operate for cause.

### B. Supervision of Activities
The DSA supervises, coordinates, and regulates the establishment, operation, and activities of all duly recognized campus clubs and organizations.

### C. Approval of Club Activities
The **Vice President for Higher Education** approves all campus club activities.

## Application for Recognition

Any group of **at least fifteen (15) members** may apply to OSA to organize a club or organization. Approval depends on:

1. A **concept paper** and **letter of application** filed at OSA, stating club objectives, signed by the fifteen (15) founding members, with the club's tentative activities for the year.
2. **Constitution and By-laws** signed and approved by officers and moderators.
3. **List of officers and members** (minimum 15 registered).
4. **Plan and Calendar of Activities**.
5. **Name of Moderator(s)**.

### F. Greek-Lettered Organizations
**The University BANS Greek-lettered organizations or other secret societies.**

## Renewal and Dissolution

### G. Renewal of Recognition
A **yearly renewal** of recognition is required and announced by OSA. Recognition and registration is a precondition for University operation and is in effect for **one (1) school year**. Clubs that do not renew are **automatically considered inactive or defunct**.

### H. Requirements for Renewal

1. Constitution and By-laws
2. List of Officers and Members
3. Plan of Activities for the current school year (including a **Program on Environmental Care**)
4. Name of Moderator(s)/Advisers
5. Report on Accomplished Activities (**80% of the proposed activities**) of the past school year
6. **Attendance at OSA Leadership Camp**
7. Attendance at all OSA and University-initiated activities
8. Accomplished **mid-year and year-end evaluation** by OSA

### I. Dissolution
The President and Moderator may apply for **voluntary dissolution** through formal notification to the DSA. All financial and property claims and clearances must be secured before OSA authorizes a clearance.

## Membership

### J. Membership Requirements

1. Only **bonafide students** are eligible.
2. Students must be on **full-time status with at least twelve (12) units** (except in the last semester).
3. Must not have **academic or disciplinary cases**.
4. Must have a **GPA of at least 1.5** and **no failing marks**.
5. **Officers must have a GPA of at least 2.0**.
6. The University reserves the right to limit or suspend participation if detrimental to academic progress.

### K. Major Positions
**No student shall hold two (2) major positions** in 2 or more clubs/organizations. Includes: president, chairman, vice president, vice chairman (internal and external).

## Reporting and Meetings

### L. Club Reports and Other Requirements

1. **Activity report** of programs/projects implemented
2. **Evaluation report**
3. **Semestral Financial Report** (Monitoring and clearance)
4. **Year-end Financial Report** (Clearance)

Failure to submit results in **clearance of officers being placed in abeyance** until submitted.

### M. Annual Meeting
All presidents of academic clubs must attend the annual meeting convened by the DSA. If the President cannot attend, the **Vice President or duly authorized representative** must be appointed (written authorization from the President required).

## Moderators

### N. Moderator/Adviser
The club submits the name of a desired moderator. The **VPHE appoints the Moderator** upon the organization's recommendation. **Term: one (1) year**, renewable.

The **VPHE appoints the moderator(s) for the Beacon Publications Office**.

**Duties of a Moderator/Adviser:**

i. Regular consultation meetings
ii. Regular attendance at club meetings
iii. Representation in all off-campus activities
iv. Periodic reporting and evaluation of the club's performance
v. Attendance at Moderators and Advisers Club Meetings

## Postings and Communications

### O. Posting of Notices and Announcements

1. Any material to be posted (on or off campus) must receive **prior clearance from the University Communications Office (UCO)** regarding content, layout, size, and duration.
2. For student organizations, announcements must bear the **OSA approval stamp**. Limited to **one (1) week** unless an extension is requested.
3. Upon UCO clearance, the VP for Administration approves printing and endorses to PPO for installation.
4. **Prohibited postings:** advertisements from companies, political propaganda, and other notices determined by OSA.
5. Postings must only be placed in **prescribed areas and assigned bulletin boards**. Painted walls, doors, windows, and trees should NOT be used. Non-compliant postings are removed and the club is banned from posting until further notice.
6. Streamers, handbills, flyers, posters must be cleared with OSA:
   - **Flyers/Handbills:** Limited to **10 per activity** (more by request).
   - **Printed copy** highly recommended. NO postings in classrooms.
   - **Posters:** ½ cartolina or ¼ Manila Paper size; **5 per activity** unless requested otherwise.
   - **Double-sided and packaging tapes are prohibited.** Use push pins.
   - Defacing, mutilating, or removing posters → disciplinary sanctions.

## Finances

### P. Organizational Funds

1. Reasonable amounts for membership and other fees (as provided in the constitution) may be collected.
2. Membership fees and other income must be reflected in **semestral and year-end financial statements**, noted by the Moderator.
3. The **Finance Office may be requested as the official depository** for large sums, with approval from the University Treasurer.
4. Academic organizations (**SLAAO, SITEAO, NAO, EAO, AAO, MAO**) must strictly follow their respective organizations' financial procedures.
5. Failure to comply → **clearance of all officers placed "on hold"**. For 4th-year graduating students, this includes **TOR and Diploma clearance**.

### Q. Fundraising Campaigns, Solicitations, Tie-ups

1. Major and minor fundraising must be noted by the Moderator and DSA, and **approved by both the VPHE and the VP for Administration**.
2. **No formalization of negotiations or agreements with external clients** without prior approval of the Moderator, VPHE, and VP for Administration.
3. **Solicitation letters** are approved by the Moderator, noted by the VPHE, and given final approval by the VP for Administration. Approved letters must be **numbered**, with a sample submitted to OSA.
4. **Solicitation reply slips and collections** must be remitted to the Treasurer **within 24 hours**. Any loss must be reported immediately with a **notarized affidavit**.
5. **Sale and distribution of food products** for fundraising must be approved by the **VP for Administration** before implementation.

## Representation and Activities

### R. Representing the University

1. Students may participate as representatives **only with verbal or written authorization from the VPHE**.
2. Transacting business using the school's name requires written authorization from the VPHE.
3. **Press releases and public notices** of school/student activities require **clearance from UCO and the VPHE**.
4. Recognized clubs may express positions on national issues with **VPHE approval**. The position must explicitly state it is the **organization's view, NOT the University's**.

### S. Extra and Co-Curricular Activities

1. **Co-curricular Activities** (lectures, symposia, seminars, exhibits) — Must be coordinated with OSA. Recommended to plan at the start of the semester. Includes field trips, academic-related practicum, company visits, and out-of-town travel.
2. **Extra-curricular Activities** (varsity teams, debating teams, etc.):
   - Must carry **full-time loads** (except seniors in their last semester).
   - Maintain a **GPA of at least 2.0**.
   - No major disciplinary record.
   - **Two (2) failing marks** make the student ineligible for the following semester.
3. All extra-curricular activities follow on/off-campus rules and regulations.
4. **NO activities a week before midterm and final examinations.**

## Activity Guidelines

### T. In-Campus Activities

1. The Moderator submits a **written request to the VPHE** with date, time, place, participants, and purpose.
2. For **overnight/overtime stays** (after 8:30 PM), members must submit a **Parent's Consent and Waiver Form** (available at OSA).
3. The **VP for Administration**, through the **University Security Chief**, approves the in-campus stay. The approval letter is forwarded to AdZU security. **Approval must be secured at least three (3) days before the event**.
4. In emergencies, AdZU security personnel must be informed for immediate assistance.

### U. Off-Campus Activities

1. The Moderator submits a written request to the **DSA** with date, time, place, participants, and purpose.
2. Submit a duly signed **Parent's Consent and Waiver Form (PC Form 1-A)**. Parents must be notified of approximate end time, and participants must be turned over to parents/guardians after.
3. The **University Security Chief approves the site/venue** before implementation.
4. **Approval must be secured at least one (1) week before scheduled implementation**. AdZU vehicles use coordinated with PPO.
5. The college student handbook applies. Emergency contact info must be on file.
6. The University is **NOT liable** for injuries from accidents (human error, equipment failure, force majeure) during transport, nor for participants leaving the official venue without authorized permission.

### V. Out-of-Town Travel

1. The Moderator submits a written request to the **VPHE** with date, time, place, participants, and purpose.
2. Submit duly signed **Parent's Consent and Waiver Form (PC Form 1-B)**. Students without the form may **NOT join**.
3. The Moderator must accompany participants at all times.
4. The approved itinerary must be **strictly followed**. Deviations only for valid reasons (weather, road conditions). If necessary, the itinerary must be signed by all participants and **notarized**. The **University Security Head must clear the site/venue**.
5. OSA must receive a copy of the approved letter, list of participants, parents' consent, and itinerary.
6. The college student handbook applies. Emergency contacts kept on file.
7. The University is **NOT liable** for injuries from accidents during transport, nor for participants leaving the venue without permission.
8. **Precautionary Safety Measures:**
   - Consider club's financial standing. Off-campus activities should be **within city limits (7-km radius)** if possible.
   - Safety is paramount. The buddy system and periodic checks are encouraged.
   - **Separate rooms/sleeping quarters for males and females.**
   - **First aid kits** readily available; know nearest hospital, police station, emergency numbers.
   - Strenuous activities may require **medical clearances**.
   - Strict adherence to the itinerary.

## W. Reservation and Use of Campus Facilities

1. Officers/student representatives, with their moderators, may request authorization from OSA and the concerned office. **First come, first served.** Official reservation forms must be filed early.
2. Priority for facility use is given to **University students, faculty, and staff**. Organizations charging admission/registration fees must pay a percentage of the hourly rate, plus any costs for electricity, generators, overtime maintenance, and security personnel.
3. Users are responsible for **upkeep and cleanliness**. Equipment permanently assigned to facilities must remain there. Misuse → liability and penalties.
4. Reservation Offices:
