# Protection of Minors and Students (CODI)

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Appendix D
**Full Title:** Committee on Decorum and Investigation (CODI) — Protocols on the Protection of Minors and Students

## Rationale

As an institution responsible for educating and nurturing young minds, AdZU is dedicated to safeguarding the well-being of its students.

- **As a Filipino institution**, AdZU upholds the right to a safe learning and working environment for minors and students, as required by Philippine laws, CHED memoranda and circulars, the University's administrative manual, and the unit's faculty and student handbook.
- **As a Catholic University**, AdZU faithfully upholds the Gospel values and advocates for the dignity of every human person and the dignity of work.
- **As a Jesuit University**, AdZU expects its members to joyfully live out the Ignatian Values — *Magis*, *Cura Personalis*, Companionship, Social Involvement, Discernment, and Animo — at all times.

## Objectives

1. **Implement systems** to ensure a safe learning and working environment for students and minors, both on and off campus, during classes, and in all activities.
2. **Equip personnel** with knowledge and skills for the protection and welfare of minors and students through annual orientations, workshops, and distribution of relevant protocols.
3. **Establish administrative processes and grievance procedures** allowing reporting and addressing violations while upholding due process under Philippine laws, CHED memoranda, the University's administrative manual, and the unit's faculty and student handbook.

## Section 3: Committee on Decorum and Investigation (CODI)

The CODI is an **ad hoc committee created by the VPHE**, who appoints its members. As part of due process, the VPHE may convene CODI for a comprehensive investigation.

**Per Article IV Section 17(C) of RA 11313**, a woman heads the committee; not less than half of its members must be women.

**Per Rule VIII Section 33 of the IRR**, for educational institutions, CODI shall include at least one representative each from school administration, trainers/instructors/professors/coaches, and students/trainees/parents. Equal representation of persons of diverse SOGIESC is emphasized as far as practicable.

### Composition

a. **Chair:** Dean (woman)
b. Formation Council Representative (woman)
c. Faculty Club President
d. Non-Academic Staff President
e. Student Government President
f. Guidance Counselor (**non-voting**)
g. **Consultant:** University Legal Counsel

If a case is filed by a student against another student, or a student against a faculty/staff member, or vice versa, the **Director of Student Affairs serves as the secretary**.

All votes are cast **orally with explanation**. Only in case of a tie does the **Chair cast a vote**.

### Functions

a. Initiate **information dissemination programs** for the protection of minors, students, and personnel from abuses listed in Article 1, Section 4 of RA 11313.
b. Monitor and evaluate strict observance of protocols.
c. Conduct **comprehensive investigation** of cases.
d. **Organize and preside over hearings**.
e. Consult the school's **legal counsel** on committee decisions.
f. Recommend to the VPHE **possible sanctions** as stipulated in the Student Handbook or Faculty Manual.

## Offenses and Sanctions (Student vs. Student)

### Offenses Carrying TWO-WEEK SUSPENSION (Minimum)

#### A. Verbal

a. Cursing, wolf-whistling, catcalling, taunting
b. Misogynistic, transphobic, homophobic, and sexist slurs
c. Persistent unwanted comments on one's body, dress, appearance, or sexual activities
d. Relentless requests for one's details (name, contact number, social media, destination)
e. Unwanted invitations, pestering for dates (in person, mail, telephone, internet, SMS, telecommunication on/off-campus)
f. Words, gestures, or actions ridiculing based on sex, gender, sexual orientation, identity, expression — including sexist, homophobic, transphobic statements; persistent sexual jokes, sexual names, comments, and demands
g. Intimidating/demeaning remarks based on gender stereotypes (not necessarily sexually motivated)
h. Any statement invading personal space or threatening personal safety

#### B. Visual

a. Unwelcome sexually suggestive gestures (or sounds) — including throwing kisses
b. Malicious leering or ogling
c. Persistent display of sexually suggestive objects, pictures, publications, audio, or video recordings unrelated to educational purposes

### Offenses Carrying DISMISSAL

a. **Making offensive hand or body gestures** at someone
b. **Exposing private parts** for sexual gratification — flashing, public masturbation, groping, similar sexual actions

### Offenses Carrying EXPULSION

a. **Stalking** (including cyberstalking) accompanied by touching, pinching, or brushing against the body
b. **Touching, pinching, or brushing** against the genitalia, face, arms, anus, groin, breasts, inner thighs, buttocks, or any body part — even when not accompanied by the above
c. **Sexual assault**
d. **Threats or insinuations** that employment, wages, academic grades, promotional opportunities, classroom/work assignments, or other conditions may be adversely affected by not submitting to sexual advances

## Due Process

Upon receiving a report, the HEU conducts a comprehensive investigation under the VPHE's mandate.

- **Student vs. Student** — Depending on gravity, the HE CODI handles the investigation.
- **Faculty/Staff vs. Student (or vice versa)** — OSA notifies the VPHE, Director of HRADO, and University President. The **University CODI** handles the investigation.

## How to Initiate Complaints

Any suspected, possible, or impending sexual harassment, misconduct, or inappropriate behavior may be reported formally or informally.

- **Formal Report** — Written complaint submitted to OSA by the person experiencing the behavior. OSA facilitates the complaint process.
- **Informal Report** — Verbal or written communication made by any person in authority within the school. The person in authority must report to OSA for initial investigation.

## A. Student Against Another Student

### Initial Investigation

1. When a student/group reports an incident involving students, **OSA has the right to conduct an initial investigation**.
2. **Incident reports must all be written**, submitted to OSA by any university personnel, parent, or guardian.
3. OSA observes due process. A decision is made within **two (2) working days or less** upon receipt of the complaint.
4. If OSA finds **no probable cause** → report submitted to VPHE (noted by the Assistant to the President for Formation) that the case is closed. CGCO addresses emotional/psychological well-being. Parents/guardians notified by counselors.
5. If the case warrants a **more comprehensive investigation** → OSA writes findings and recommendations to the VPHE.

### Investigation Proper

1. The **VPHE convenes the CODI**.
2. CODI observes due process. Decision made within **ten (10) working days or less** after the VPHE convenes the committee.
3. CODI writes findings and recommendations to the VPHE.
4. VPHE communicates findings to parents/guardians **within two (2) working days**. A received copy signed by parents/guardians is kept in OSA.
5. Parents/guardians have the **right to appeal within five (5) working days** after receiving the correspondence.

### Appeal

1. When an appeal is made, the case goes to the VPHE.
2. VPHE has **five (5) working days** to review the entire process.
3. After comprehensive evaluation, VPHE communicates findings and recommendations to parents/guardians. The same report goes to the **University President**.
4. If still unsatisfied, **one last appeal to the University President**.
5. The University President reserves the right to **reopen the case at any time**.
6. **The decision of the University President is final and executory.**

### Remedies and Psychological Welfare

After the decision is executed, the **University Chaplain** ensures that the psycho-emotional needs of both the complainant and the accused are not neglected.

---

## B. Student Against Faculty/Staff OR Faculty/Staff Against Student

### Initial Investigation

1. **Incident reports must all be written**, furnished to the VPHE and the Assistant to the President for Formation.
2. OSA observes due process. Decision made within **two (2) working days or less**.
3. OSA reports findings and recommendations (per Student Handbook or Faculty Manual) to the VPHE. If the case merits CODI involvement, OSA includes it as a recommendation.
4. The VPHE sends correspondence to parents/guardians and university personnel. Received copies kept:
   - Parents/guardians' signed copy → OSA
   - University personnel's signed copy → **201 file**
   - Forwarded to VPHE and Director of HRADO

### Investigation Proper

1. The VPHE notifies the **Director of HRADO** to convene the CODI.
2. CODI decision within **ten (10) working days or less** after VPHE convenes the committee.
3. CODI writes findings and recommendations to the VPHE.
4. VPHE communicates findings within **two (2) working days**. Received copies kept in OSA (parents/guardians) and **201 file** (faculty/staff). Same report forwarded to VPHE and HRADO Director.
5. Right to appeal within **five (5) working days**.

### Appeal

Same five-step process as Student vs. Student, with University President's decision being final and executory.

### Remedies and Psychological Welfare

University Chaplain ensures conditions and psycho-emotional needs of both parties are not neglected.

- **Resource speakers** may be invited to discuss Philippine laws, memoranda, circulars, Professional Code of Ethics for Teachers, the administrative manual, faculty manual, and student handbook.
- Modules may be prepared in coordination with the AP for Formation and HE units for FFP discussion.

## Orientation and Workshops

Conducted by the **Assistant to the President for Formation** or the **VPHE**, in partnership with **FFP**, in collaboration with relevant offices. **All HE personnel oriented annually**. Continuous programs/workshops for all stakeholders.

## Sanctions (Summary)

The protocols implement the sanctions stipulated in:

- The **Student Handbook**
- The **Administrative Manual**
- The **Faculty Manual**

If the violation is not listed, a penalty commensurate with the violation is imposed.

**For students:** verbal or written reprimands, community service, suspension, dismissal, expulsion, and other appropriate penalties.

**For faculty and staff:** verbal or written reprimands, suspension, dismissal, and other appropriate penalties.

## Section 4: Reporting Mechanisms

### A. Procedures for Reporting

Any person who believes they have been subjected to prohibited behavior may file a complaint with **OSA**, formally in writing or informally.

#### Formal Complaint Requirements

A **written complaint** to OSA, **signed by the complainant**, containing:

a. Name of the complainant
b. Date of the incident(s)
c. Description of the incident(s)
d. Name of the alleged offender(s)
e. Any supporting evidence or witnesses (if any)

#### Informal Complaint

Verbal or written communication to any person in authority (faculty, staff, administrators). The person in authority must report it to OSA for an initial investigation.

### B. Confidentiality and Privacy

All reports and complaints are treated with the **strictest confidentiality possible**. Information is only disclosed to individuals who need to know to facilitate investigation and resolution.

### C. Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against any individual who reports a violation in good faith or participates in an investigation is **strictly prohibited**. Any retaliation will be subject to disciplinary action.

### D. False Reports

Knowingly making a **false report** or providing **false information** during an investigation is a violation of these protocols and may result in disciplinary action.

## Section 5: Support and Remedies

### A. Support for Complainants

The University provides support, including:

- Referrals to counseling services
- Medical services
- Academic support services

### B. Remedies for Complainants

The University takes appropriate action:

- Issuing no-contact orders
- Providing academic accommodations
- Taking disciplinary action against the alleged offender

### C. Support for Respondents

- Referrals to counseling services
- Legal services

### D. Remedies for Respondents

If a complaint is found false or unsubstantiated, the University will:

- Expunge the complaint from the respondent's record
- Provide counseling services as appropriate

## Section 6: Record Keeping and Reporting

- **OSA maintains records** of all reports and complaints, kept confidential to the extent permitted by law.
- **Annual report** by OSA to the VPHE and CODI summarizing the number/types of reports, investigation outcomes, and actions taken.

## Section 7: Training and Education

- **Training** on protocols for all members of the university community (administrators, faculty, staff, students). Includes prevention, reporting mechanisms, support and remedies.
- **Education** on prevention of protocol violations — sexual harassment, gender-based discrimination, importance of creating a safe and inclusive campus environment.

## Section 8: Review and Revision

Protocols are reviewed and revised as necessary in consultation with **CODI and relevant stakeholders**.

## Section 9: Conclusion

These protocols promote the safety and well-being of all minors and students at AdZU. They reflect the University's commitment to creating a campus environment free from discrimination, harassment, and other misconduct. All members are encouraged to familiarize themselves with these protocols and report any violations promptly.
