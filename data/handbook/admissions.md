# Admissions

**Source:** AdZU College Student Handbook, Chapter 2 (Academic Regulations)

## General Admission Policy

1. Admission to the University is open to qualified young men and women. It does not discriminate based on race, ethnic background, religion, color, national ancestry, sex, age, disability, or employment status — except as stipulated in an applicable CHED Memorandum Order (CMO).
2. Admission for all courses is determined principally by the results of the **College Entrance Test (CET)**. The minimum percentile rank for admission to the University is **30% or higher**.
3. Admission requirements are not the same for all degree programs. A student who qualifies for admission to one program may not be admitted to another if they do not meet that program's requirements. Board programs (those with licensure examinations) require **higher minimum CET percentile rankings**. Nursing applicants must be **physically and mentally fit**.
4. International or foreign students must meet both the University's requirements and the applicable government regulations.
5. The University accepts cross-enrollees from other schools, auditors, and special students, subject to University conditions.
6. An Ateneo student who transfers to another school and wishes to return is **not automatically readmitted**. They must reapply as a transfer student under transfer requirements.
7. The University reserves the right to refuse admission to any student at any time for reasons of **unsatisfactory grades and/or undesirable conduct**.

## Transfer Students

A small number of transfer students are accepted each semester. Admission is determined by CET results and GPA scores from previous semesters at another school.

- To obtain an Ateneo diploma, a transfer student must have **resided at Ateneo for at least three (3) years** or have **taken at least 75% of the total courses**.
- As a general policy, transfer students seeking admission into the **senior year are NOT accepted**.
- Admission requirements for transferees in board programs will be applied accordingly.
- To transfer credit for a course, the student must have earned at least a grade of **C or 3.00** — or **one full grade higher than the passing grade** in the previous institution's grading system. The relevant department determines any additional requirements (e.g., validation exam).
- Credits earned for **PATHFIT** (equivalent to Physical Education) and the **National Service Training Program (NSTP)** may be validated using the same procedures as for academic courses.
- To be eligible for validation, courses must have been taken **within four years** before the student's transfer to AdZU.

## Transfer of Credits from Other Institutions

Transfer of Credit is the process of validating and granting credit for courses completed at another institution.

1. Credit may be granted if AdZU offers an equivalent course based on the prospectus of the degree program. Free electives do not require an equivalent.
2. **Philosophy and Religious Studies courses should generally be taken at AdZU.** Credits may be granted only if equivalent courses are taken from other Ateneo institutions.
3. **Thesis and capstone courses** must be undertaken and completed in residence at AdZU.
4. Grades earned from transferred credits are **not included** in the computation of the student's GPA.
5. The grade reflected on the student's transcript for a transferred course will be the **original grade** assigned by the institution where the course was completed.

## Cross-Enrollment to Other Schools

A student may cross-enroll in a course at another college or university while actively enrolled at AdZU, provided ALL the following conditions are met:

1. The student is a **graduating senior**.
2. The course is **not** in Religious Studies, Philosophy, or one of the AdZU core courses.
3. The course is **not offered at AdZU** during the specific semester.
4. The combined total units (at AdZU + cross-enrolled) must **not exceed the maximum load** for that program.
5. Approval must be obtained from **both** the Home Department Chairperson **and** the Department Chairperson responsible for validating the cross-enrolled course.
6. The course must have an equivalent in AdZU with a corresponding course code in the program prospectus.
7. The student must cross-enroll only in an **accredited university**.

**Note:** Endorsement does not guarantee validation. The validating department may require a minimum final grade or validation exam.

## Cross-Enrollment FROM Other Institutions to AdZU

Students from other schools wishing to cross-enroll at AdZU must:

1. Submit a cross-enrollment permit with required documents to the **Admissions & Aid Office (OAA)**.
2. Ensure course equivalency — similar title, description, and number of credit units.
3. Register the course at the **College Registrar's Office**.
4. Pay assessed tuition and all applicable fees at the **Finance Office** to be validated for the current term.

## Shifting to Another Program

Students may shift to other programs provided they meet the requirements set by the receiving department.

1. Students are eligible to shift only after completing their **first year of studies** at AdZU.
2. Students must complete the **Program Change Form** (available through the MyAdZU portal).
3. The completed form must be signed by the relevant offices and submitted by the student to the receiving department.

## Re-Admission

An Ateneo student who left and now wishes to return is treated as a **transfer student** and must reapply under transfer admission requirements.
